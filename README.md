# Consumer_shopping
Consumer shopping trend analysis
Project Details:
Here in this project, I have to analyze the purchasing habits and shopping habits of the customers based on several factors ( age , income, gender, etc.).  

For this, I worked on the external site data (~ 12,000 customers) and imported the .csv file to Excel . 

The data consists of 27 columns, and I performed data cleaning using Power Query . Additionally , within this data sheet , I inserted 5 extra columns using Excel formulas (with IF and AND operators ) for further analysis of the data. 

I applied 9 separate pivot tables to extract the reports based on the required parameters.  

The reports that I analyzed are as follows : 

1. Report showing age group bifurcation as per online spend and average store spend.
2. Report showing average online spend as per income group.
3. Report showing average online spend and store spend based on daily internet hours usage.
4. Report showing average online spend and store spend based on daily social hours usage.
5. Report showing monthly online orders based on daily social hours usage.
6. Report showing monthly store visits based on daily internet hours usage.
7. Report showing product return frequency based on monthly online orders .
8. Report showing average store spend based on city and shopping preferences .
9. Report showing average store spend based on gender.

I also inserted 4 slicers (Daily_Int_Hrs_bif, Monthly_order_bracket , Age_bif, Income_bracket) for filtering the results inside the reports on the "Online Shopping Trends" dashboard. 

Additionally, I included (1 clustered chart, 1 combo chart, 3 bar charts , 2 pie charts, and 1 line chart) in the dashboard for reporting.

Insights --------------------------------------------------------------- 
1. Findings: Customers using fewer internet hours are ordering less.
Suggestion: Enable one-click reordering for essential items, allowing them to reorder without browsing the full site. Eliminate mandatory account creation for the first few orders to reduce barriers. Ensure the website is highly responsive and loads quickly on mobile devices, even with slower connections .

2. Findings: Customers spending fewer hours on social media are ordering less.
Suggestion: Ensure your app or website has a one-click checkout, easy search, and high-quality visuals, minimizing the time between product discovery and purchase.  Offer instant support via AI chatbots to answer questions immediately, reducing the "helplessness" some customers feel when shopping online . Because of higher perceived risks associated with reduced social media exposure, use and showcase testimonials, reviews, and user-generated content (UGC) on your product pages to build confidence.

3. Findings: Customers placing average orders are returning more items .
Suggestion: Utilize high-resolution images (at least five per product), 360-degree views, and videos to set accurate expectations. Create detailed, interactive size charts, include model measurements, and showcase user-generated photos. Encourage customers to exchange items rather than request a full refund, keeping the revenue.

4. Findings: Customers spending fewer internet hours are visiting fewer stores.
Suggestion: Implement seamless inventory management across platforms (buy online, pick up in-store). Ensure branding is consistent across both digital and physical touchpoints to build trust. Use QR codes in physical stores or catalogs to allow quick, easy access to product details or to complete purchases online.

5. Findings: Customers in lower income brackets are ordering less.
Suggestion: Low-income customers often prefer buying in bulk for essentials to stretch their budget. Offer bundled, lower-cost packs of essential items (e.g., household essentials, non-perishable food).
